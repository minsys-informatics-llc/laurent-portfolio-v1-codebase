Leadership Under Pressure — Two Key Decisions
Decision 1 — IP protection vs. quant team demand: At month 4, the quantitative team insisted on exposing their full model parameter space through the API. I pushed back — this created IP leakage risk and an unsustainable support burden. I proposed a "curated output" model with versioned, pre-configured endpoints. The quant lead escalated to the CTO; I walked through three client scenarios proving the curated approach covered 95% of demand without exposing a single proprietary parameter. The CTO backed the model — it became the firm's standard API productization pattern.
Decision 2 — Compliance-first sequencing: I prioritized the MiFID II compliance layer 3 months ahead of client-facing analytics, over the product team's push to "show momentum" with features first. The supervisory review was existential — a failed audit would freeze everything. Delivering compliance first meant every subsequent product launched already audit-ready, eliminating the costly retrofitting cycle.
