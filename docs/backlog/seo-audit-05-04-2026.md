## Context & Diagnosis

Your site is a **Vite + React 19 SPA** (single-page application). This creates a **fundamental tension** with SEO: Googlebot and most AI crawlers see the raw HTML *before* JavaScript executes. Your tab components (Career Timeline, Skills) hide half your content behind interactive state — content that may be **completely invisible** to crawlers. That is the #1 issue to solve.

---

## Audit Controls — What, Why & How

### 1. **JavaScript-Rendered Content Visibility** *(Critical)*

**What:** Audit what content is accessible to crawlers that do not interact with your Career Timeline tabs (Corporate / Entrepreneurial) and Skills tabs (Hard / Soft).

**Why:** Google's crawl-render pipeline does execute JS, but it does so in a second wave (hours to days later), at lower priority, and it **never clicks tabs**. AI crawlers (ClaudeBot, GPTBot, PerplexityBot) generally scrape static HTML. Your `noscript` block exists but only covers the top-level headings — it does **not** include the actual timeline entries, skill cards, or case study dashboards inside the tabs.

**How:**
- Read the `noscript` block in `index.html` and compare with what's rendered inside the tab components in `Home.tsx`
- Identify which content is inside `AnimatePresence` / conditionally rendered tab panels
- Determine the gap between crawler-visible content vs. actual content
- Check whether `display:none` or DOM removal is used (DOM removal = invisible to Google)

---

### 2. **Structured Data (JSON-LD) Completeness & Accuracy** *(High)*

**What:** Audit the existing `Person + ProfilePage` schema and identify missing schema types needed for LLM knowledge graph embedding.

**Why:** To embed your professional brand into LLM knowledge graphs (what you called "LLM Graph knowledges"), you need **entity richness**. Google's Knowledge Graph and AI training datasets rely on structured data to understand *who you are*, *what you've done*, and *where you've worked*. Your current schema is minimal — it's missing work history, specific expertise claims, and links between entities.

**How:**
- Read the current JSON-LD block in `index.html`
- Validate against schema.org Person spec
- Identify missing properties: `worksFor`, `alumniOf`, `hasOccupation`, `award`, `workExample`, `knowsLanguage`, `nationality`
- Evaluate whether to add `ItemList` schema for career entries or `Article` schema for case studies
- Check `sameAs` link quality (LinkedIn + Minsys — are they canonical profile URLs?)

---

### 3. **Sitemap Quality & Section Coverage** *(High)*

**What:** Audit `sitemap.xml` for completeness and evaluate whether section-level anchors should be represented.

**Why:** Your sitemap only contains the root URL. While this is technically correct for a SPA, it tells Google nothing about the depth of your content. For Google Search Console to index your portfolio effectively, the sitemap must be accurate, valid, and submitted.

**How:**
- Read `sitemap.xml` and validate structure (XML schema, dates, priorities)
- Verify the `lastmod` date is meaningful (currently 2026-04-04 — is this accurate?)
- Evaluate whether anchor-based section URLs (e.g., `/#career-timeline`) should be added
- Check that the sitemap URL in `robots.txt` is accessible

---

### 4. **robots.txt & Crawler Permissions** *(Medium)*

**What:** Audit `robots.txt` for correctness, completeness, and AI crawler coverage.

**Why:** Your `robots.txt` is ambitious — it explicitly lists ~12 AI crawlers. This is good intent, but the file needs to be validated: wrong syntax blocks crawlers silently. The goal is that *every* web crawler and AI scraper can fully access all your content.

**How:**
- Read `robots.txt` in full
- Validate syntax (User-agent / Allow / Disallow / Sitemap directives)
- Check known AI crawler user-agent strings for accuracy (some may be outdated or misspelled)
- Verify no accidental `Disallow` rules

### 5. **llms.txt & AI Knowledge Graph Optimization** *(High)*

**What:** Audit `llms.txt` and `llms-full.txt` for content richness, entity clarity, and discoverability by AI training pipelines.

**Why:** The `llms.txt` standard (proposed by Jeremy Howard / fast.ai) is being adopted by AI companies to help LLMs understand a site's content. PerplexityBot, ClaudeBot, and similar crawlers may consume this file. For your brand to be embedded in LLM knowledge, this file must contain **dense, unambiguous, entity-rich prose** — not marketing copy.

**How:**
- Read both `llms.txt` and `llms-full.txt`
- Evaluate information density: Is your full career timeline included? All skills? All case studies with metrics?
- Check entity disambiguation (full name, LinkedIn URL, Minsys URL explicitly stated)
- Identify missing data that's in the React components but not in the text files
- Evaluate formatting (LLMs prefer clean, structured prose with clear sections)

### 6. **On-Page SEO — Title, Meta, Headings & Content** *(High)*

**What:** Audit the core on-page signals: `<title>`, `<meta description>`, `<h1>–<h3>` hierarchy, keyword targeting.

**Why:** These are the signals Google weighs most heavily for ranking. Your title is "Laurent Vincentelli | Professional Portfolio" — while correct, "Professional Portfolio" is a low-value generic phrase. Your target keywords should be your actual professional identity terms.

**How:**
- Read `index.html` title and meta description
- Audit all heading tags (`h1`, `h2`, `h3`) in `Home.tsx` for hierarchy and keyword usage
- Evaluate if your primary expertise terms ("Digital Platforms Lead", "AI Platforms", "Platform Engineering") appear in headings
- Check character counts (title ≤ 60 chars, description ≤ 160 chars)
- Verify `lang="en"` is set on `<html>`

### 7. **Open Graph & Social Card Completeness** *(Medium)*

**What:** Audit OG and Twitter card meta tags for completeness and image validity.

**Why:** When your profile is shared on LinkedIn, Twitter/X, or used as a reference by AI tools, the OG data is what gets rendered. A missing or broken OG image kills credibility.

**How:**
- Read all OG and Twitter meta tags in `index.html`
- Verify `og:image` URL resolves to an actual image with correct dimensions (1200×630px recommended)
- Check `og:type` (should be `profile` for a personal portfolio, not `website`)
- Validate `og:image:alt` is present (accessibility + AI parsing)

### 8. **Image SEO — Alt Text & Performance** *(Medium)*

**What:** Audit image alt attributes, lazy loading, and optimization.

**Why:** Alt text is read by search engines and AI crawlers to understand image context. Your profile photo and case study images are part of your professional story — missing or weak alt text wastes SEO value.

**How:**
- Find all `<img>` tags in all components
- Audit alt text quality: is it descriptive and keyword-relevant?
- Check `loading="lazy"` usage (good for performance, but should not be on above-the-fold profile photo)
- Evaluate whether Unsplash external images should be replaced with local/self-hosted assets
- Check if `og:image` is a local file (it should be, for reliability)

### 9. **Technical SEO — Favicon, Canonical, Performance** *(Medium)*

**What:** Audit technical basics: favicon, canonical tag, HTTPS, page speed indicators.

**Why:** Missing favicon signals to Google that the site is incomplete. Canonical tag prevents duplicate content issues. Page speed is a ranking factor (Core Web Vitals).

**How:**
- Check `index.html` for `<link rel="icon">`, `<link rel="apple-touch-icon">`
- Verify canonical tag format and URL
- Check for `<meta name="theme-color">` (important for mobile Chrome)
- Review `vite.config.ts` for any performance optimizations (code splitting, preload hints)
- Check `<link rel="preconnect">` for Google Fonts (already likely present)
- Note Google Analytics stub is not configured (placeholder ID `G-XXXXXXXXXX`)

**Why:** For personal branding to rank and to be embedded in AI knowledge graphs, Google's Quality Rater Guidelines and LLM training pipelines look for **verifiable authority signals**: real name, real employer links, dated work history, real credentials. Unverifiable claims are ignored or downweighted.
